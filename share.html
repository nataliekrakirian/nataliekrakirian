Stuck in the middle
The story
I am the middle child of 3 girls. My older sister tends to hide her insecurities while my younger sister constantly vocalizes her dissatisfaction with her body. I tend to wonder why there is such a big difference with the way they deal with their insecurities, I mean it could be an age thing or personality thing. But, what I did learn was my younger sister thinks or believes she is not what she should look like because it's not "what she sees" on Instagram. She, being so heavily influenced by instagram and this new "healthy trend" (a.k.a be skinny until your ribs show or until your legs don't touch) has more to deal with then we have before. Though it's been there for decades it's reaching a younger crowd now. I had to sit her down and explain to her that not everybody's body is built to have their legs separated, it's just not a thing. She began to compare herself to me and my body, and it made me feel guilty. I mean she's my baby sister, I'd rather deal with it then her. I reassured her we just have different body types and she is just so lucky to be healthy, pointing out all the good things about her body. I frequently remind her of these things and make suggestions on how to view the topic. I hope it's helping, but who knows, social media may interfere with my efforts.
Every family reunion ever
The story
I went to my grandparent's house for a joint birthday party between my grandfather and my aunt. My aunt was visiting from Maine and the family hadn't seen her in years. A large part of the party was celebrating my aunt's being back and finally getting to spend time with us. When it came time to open gifts my grandmother's tact started to go out the window. My aunt opened a sweater that was slightly too large for her and she tried to make the best of the situation and offered that she can cinch it around her waist with a belt. My grandma then proceeded to tell her that she was getting too big for that and she wouldn't be able to fit the belt around. Throughout the rest of the party there were multiple comments made about how much she was eating and what she was eating. This was really hard to listen to because we rarely get to see my aunt and she is such a wonderful beautiful person but my grandmother managed to bring her down with rude comments that she didn't even realize affected any of us.
It's never enough
The story
To start off, I’m a guy. I can honestly say that I have never been confident in my appearance. I was chubby when I was in elementary school, and my family was always quick to point out that if I ate healthier I wouldn’t be so chubby. Needless to say, my self esteem took a hit. After reading through this website, I realized that I constantly fat babble. I am constantly beating myself up for eating dessert, and I feel like I’m a failure if I don’t go to the gym. My friends all have a similar mindset, so I’ve never thought of it as a bad thing that could affect me negatively. I’m looking forward to trying some of the activities that were mentioned in the love section of this site.
